Tribute to Alan Turing

"Sometimes it is the people no one can imagine anything of who do the things no one can imagine"

TIMELINE

  • June 23, 1912. Turing was born in Maida Vale, London, to Julius Mathison Turing (1873–1947) and Ethel Sara Turing (née Stoney; 1881–1976)
  • 1931-1934. Turing studied as an undergraduate at King's College, Cambridge, where he was awarded first-class honours in mathematics
  • March 18, 1940. The first Bombe, an Enigma code breaker, was installed during his work at Bletchley Park, during World War II.
  • June 8, 1954. Turing was found dead at his home, from cyanide poisoning, after battling with imposed hormone therapy by the British authorities due to being homosexual.
